UK Hydrographic Office - Marine Innovation Challenge

The Admiralty Marine Innovation Programme was held virtually in March 2021, which addresses challenges and solutions related to ocean disturbances. This year’s theme revolved around “Assessing risk and resilience for coastal flooding”. My group chose to tackle the issues related to the sea level rise on Anguilla island located in the Caribbean. Within 3 weeks we developed a tool that uses satellite data and Reanalysis dataset in order to create an alert system that can be used to warn the population about hazards in that region. Our prototype was the runner-up in the program.
Team members: Renata Grassi, Felipe dos Santos, Ana Carolina Cupolilo

We developed CIRAD (Coastal Inundation Risk Assessment and Display tool). It is a GIS prototype tool, based on Reanalysis ERA5 hourly Dataset and SAR images (Radar frequency: 5405 MHz). After evaluating the critical areas on Anguilla and analyzing the netCDF data from ERA5, we set waves, wind, and precipitation values that we agreed to be potentially harmful. Using Python, we applied masks (filters) to be our alert level 1 (more dangerous) and 2 (warning alert) and applied those masks in the Anguilla´s map. The result can be seen in QGis software.
